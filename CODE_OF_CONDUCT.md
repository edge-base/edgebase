# EdgeBase Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

EdgeBase is committed to a respectful, welcoming, and constructive community.
We want contributors, users, and maintainers to feel safe participating in
issues, pull requests, discussions, docs, and other project spaces.

## Expected Behavior

- Be respectful in tone, even when you disagree.
- Focus feedback on ideas, code, and behavior, not on people.
- Assume good intent, ask clarifying questions, and stay curious.
- Share context that helps others reproduce, understand, and fix problems.
- Be generous with credit and mindful of different experience levels.
- Respect boundaries, privacy, and people's time.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, intimidation, threats, or personal attacks
- Discriminatory or demeaning language
- Trolling, dogpiling, baiting, or deliberately hostile behavior
- Publishing private or identifying information without permission
- Sexualized language or unwanted sexual attention
- Repeatedly derailing technical discussions after moderation requests
- Any other conduct that a reasonable person would consider abusive or unsafe

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- GitHub issues, pull requests, and review comments
- Documentation and website contributions
- Community discussions and support channels run by the project
- Public spaces where someone is representing EdgeBase

## Reporting

If you experience or witness behavior that violates this Code of Conduct,
please report it privately to the maintainers at `edgebase52@gmail.com`.

When possible, include:

- A description of what happened
- Links, screenshots, or other relevant context
- The usernames involved
- Any immediate safety concerns

Reports will be handled as confidentially as practical. We will review each
report in good faith and take action that we believe is appropriate to protect
the community.

## Enforcement

Project maintainers may take any action they find appropriate, including:

- Requesting a change in behavior
- Editing, hiding, or removing content
- Locking conversations
- Rejecting contributions
- Temporarily or permanently restricting participation

## Project Expectations

We are building an ambitious open-source project. Strong opinions, detailed
reviews, and direct technical feedback are welcome. Disrespect is not.
